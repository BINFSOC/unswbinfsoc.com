Our Mission

At BINFSOC, our mission is to unite students from bioinformatics as well as computer science, statistics, biology and chemistry disciplines, to create a network of future thinkers and innovators.

We aim to equip students with fundamental skills they need to excel in industry, whilst enriching the student life experience.

If Biologists Push Carts

Bioinformaticians Invent Wheels

What is Bioinformatics?

junk DNA

Emma Harding, current PhD candidate and a researcher working with Dr. Peter White, writes about how remnants of ancient viruses in marsupial cells pose very interesting questions for the future of paleovirology. By using a range of bioinformatics techniques and tools, they have been able to obtain and analyse a list of endogenous viral elements that can be used to trace viruses back thousands of years. In this issue we also discuss some recent society events and give an intro to Shell scripting and the R programming language.
